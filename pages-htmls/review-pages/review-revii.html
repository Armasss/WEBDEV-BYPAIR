    Resident Evil Village

    Made by: Mark Rainier Armas

    Introduction

    Dimitrescu Family

    When I started Resident Evil Village, I wasn’t sure how it would follow the intensity of Resident Evil 7: Biohazard. But within minutes, I realized this wasn’t just another horror game—it was an ambitious blend of survival horror and action, delivering both eerie tension and heart-pounding thrills. Capcom didn’t just continue Ethan Winters’ story; they raised the stakes in every possible way.

    Our Insights

    Chris Redfield

    Resident Evil Village masterfully balances atmospheric horror with fast-paced action. Exploring the eerie village and its terrifying locales—like Lady Dimitrescu’s castle and Heisenberg’s factory—was a constant thrill. The game’s enemies are varied and keep you on edge, and the puzzles are clever without being frustrating. What stood out to me most was the sheer variety in gameplay; no section feels repetitive. While the story does lean into the bizarre at times, it never lost my interest. My only gripe is that the game’s second half shifts more toward action, which slightly lessened the horror elements I loved early on.

    Conclusion

    Explore Castle Dimitrescu

    Resident Evil Village is a fantastic evolution of the series, offering an engaging mix of scares, exploration, and combat. Whether you’re a longtime fan or new to the franchise, it’s an unforgettable journey through one of the most atmospheric games I’ve ever played.

    FAQ

    Do I need to play Resident Evil 7: Biohazard first?

    It’s not necessary, but highly recommended since Village continues Ethan Winters’ story and builds on events from Resident Evil 7.

    How long does it take to complete Resident Evil Village?

    The main story takes about 8-12 hours, but exploring side content can extend playtime to 15-20 hours.

    Is Resident Evil Village more action or horror?

    It’s a mix, with the first half focusing on horror and tension, while the second half leans more into action.
