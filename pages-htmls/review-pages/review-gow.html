    God Of War: Ragnarok

    Made by: Mark Rainier Armas

    Introduction

    The Ghost of Sparta

    After playing God of War (2018), I couldn’t wait for the next installment. The journey of Kratos and Atreus left such a lasting impression that I found myself constantly wondering what came next. So when God of War Ragnarök finally dropped, I dove in headfirst—and it absolutely delivered. This game doesn’t just continue their story; it expands and deepens it, giving me an experience that was equal parts epic and emotional.

    Our Insights

    New Way of Travelling

    The gameplay is everything I loved about the first game but elevated. The combat feels smoother and more satisfying, with new weapons and abilities that kept me experimenting throughout. What really stood out, though, was the story. Watching Kratos wrestle with his past and trying to be a better father, while Atreus grows into his own person, hit me on an emotional level I didn’t expect. The new characters, like Thor and Odin, are fantastic too—layered and compelling in ways I didn’t see coming. If I had one tiny complaint, it’s that some of the side quests felt like they broke the pacing, but even those had rewarding moments.

    Conclusion

    Atreus and Angrboða

    For me, God of War Ragnarök isn’t just a great game—it’s an unforgettable experience that has stuck with me long after finishing it. From its breathtaking visuals to the deep, emotional narrative, the game creates a perfect balance between heart-pounding action and quiet, heartfelt moments. Every battle, every choice, feels impactful, and the growth of both Kratos and Atreus over the course of the story is something I deeply connected with. The game weaves its complex themes of family, responsibility, and destiny in a way that few games manage to do so effectively. If you loved the 2018 game, or even if you’re coming into the series for the first time, Ragnarök offers something for everyone—whether it's the thrilling combat, the expansive world, or the powerful story. It’s an experience that demands to be played, and I’m confident it will stick with you long after the credits roll.

    FAQ

    Do I need to play God of War (2018) before Ragnarök?

    While it’s not mandatory, playing God of War (2018) is highly recommended to understand the story and relationships in Ragnarök.

    How long does it take to finish God of War Ragnarök?

    The main story takes around 20-25 hours, but completing side quests and exploring can extend it to 40-50 hours.

    Is God of War Ragnarök available on PS4?

    Yes, it’s available on both PS4 and PS5, with enhanced performance and visuals on PS5.
